Our Mission

The Mission of MilitaryLawProject.com is to promote and defend the rights of military personnel, especially those serving in support of contingency operations, from dismissive bureaucratic neglect and group-think that denies individual service member their rights in U.S. law.

Background

The first major project of MilitaryLawProject.com involves a years-long effort to correct a glaring injustice against deploying reserve members based solely on the fact they volunteered for wartime active duty.

Did This Happen To You?

A class action lawsuit has been filed in federal court by MilitaryLawProject on behalf of federal civilian employees who volunteered (often under authority of 10 U.S.C. 12301(d) but if you're not sure, we encourage you to click below for more information) to serve on active duty in support of a contingency operation after 1 January 2009. Those volunteers should have been paid a differential pay benefit, sometimes in the tens of thousands per year, by their civilian agency if their military pay was less than their federal civilian pay. If you would like more information, click below and provide a few details of your tour so we can provide you more information and potential payment of past benefits.
